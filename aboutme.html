CV

A diligent, creative and thoughtful graphic editor, I have nine years of experience designing and editing data-led information graphics and I spent six years photo editing for The Wall Street Journal. Since completing General Assembly’s front-end web development course, I have enjoyed finding new and interesting ways to interactively present visual data-driven trends.

WORK EXPERIENCE

Senior Graphics Editor, The Wall Street Journal, London, October 2012-present

I create, from conception to publication, intelligent and concise information graphics for web and print publications and on towards social media. Working with reporters and editors to produce a sophisticated visualisation, I research and analyse a wide range of datasets and economic trends, with a strong emphasis on U.K. and European economics and markets. In order to present European statistics to an American and global audience, I’ve had to utilize my precise attention to detail and ability to learn and understand a wide range of metrics and concepts.

Since 2014 I’ve trained in front-end web development to conceive, pitch, design and produce complex interactive information graphics and find clean ways to quickly and effectively display static information graphics in a responsive online presence. Although new to the coding world, I bring the discipline of nine years’ experience in news-driven information graphics to the Journal’s new focus on digital visualisations. Last autumn I sat on a panel at City University London and discussed the benefits of teamwork and collaboration in unconventional journalism.

From 2012 to 2015, I was responsible for the visual integrity and news value of The Wall Street Journal’s European edition front page. On a daily basis I undertake an intense photo edit of all major photo wire services live images to present and help determine the front-page photo for the both the print and iPad editions. I utilized this experience to coordinate with the marketing team to research and select news images that represent The Wall Street Journal and Dow Jones brand identity in the News Building, 1 London Bridge Street. I have commissioned photo shoots and negotiated photo contracts on behalf of The Wall Street Journal, and manage the photo billing for the European graphics department.

Graphics Editor, The Wall Street Journal, Brussels and London, August 2008-October 2012

Working with a small team of designers and editors, I was responsible for the visual intergrity and accuracy of The Wall Street Journal Europe's print and online platforms. Balancing multiple deadlines each day, I research, coordinate, create and edit breaking-news graphics, maps and photos. As a graphics editor I am in constant communication with news editors in Europe, Asia and the U.S. about page layout, story placement, and art priority. I am also responsible for maintaining The Wall Street Journal's style in graphics. In addition to a daily workload, art direction for Weekend edition, online graphics and photos.

Information Graphic Designer, The Wall Street Journal, New York, February 2006-August 2008

Responsible for the research, analysis, and visual representation of a diverse array of data and  breaking news for the Economy, Leading the News, Corporate Focus and Money & Investing sections. A member of the Information Graphics Department requires consistent coordination with news editors, photo editors and art directors on the inquest and breakdown of stories for relevant graphics, composition of headlines and decks; all the while designing clean, concise and effective information graphics under a strict, daily deadline.
